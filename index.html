Welcome

This prototype simulates the process of verifying a customer's age when purchasing age restricted products online using payment card details. This demonstrates the four key steps involved in the process:

  1. Basket – Choice of items to be purchased from a range of age restricted and non-restricted products.
  2. MCC code – Generating of the applicable MCC code based on the items in the customer's basket.
  3. Payment – Entering payment card details, from which the customer's age is verified.
  4. Success – Confirmation that the order has been approved and the customer has passed the age verification checks.

    At this stage, you can add items to your basket including age restricted prodcuts. No attempts are made to check if age restricted products are present or the age of the user.

    When the user goes to checkout, the list of items to be purchased are collated and the purchaser's details collected. At this stage no information is known about the age of the purchaser.

    As the purchaser moves from the checkout to the payment page, the system identifies any age restricted products. This determines the MCC code, with the most restrictive code applied to the purchase. This is based on a code for grocery stores and supermarkets.

    Now the purchaser enters their payment details. The transaction details along with the MCC code are passed to the relevant banking service, who uses the date of birth information contained within their systems to either accept or decline the transaction based on the age of the purchaser. A check is also made on the name and address entered to prevent those under age from using alternative delivery points.
